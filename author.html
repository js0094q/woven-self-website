The Memoir

About the Memoir

Unfolding Origami is a trauma therapist's memoir about surviving trauma, naming coercive control, and learning to trust your own shape again.

The story traces how confusion, manipulation, and reality reversal can separate someone from their intuition, their voice, and their sense of safety.

It is also a memoir of return: surviving quietly, re-defining your shape, and moving through the long, nonlinear work of folding back to yourself.

Healing is not becoming untouched. Sometimes it is folding back to yourself and learning how to hold the crease.
