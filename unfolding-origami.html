About the Book

About the Memoir

Unfolding Origami traces a mind under pressure as it untangles from coercive environments, emotional misrecognition, and the long silence that often follows psychic injury.

Through lyrical scenes and steady reflection, the memoir follows nervous-system survival over the arc of memory loss, denial, recognition, and repair. It is grounded in the details of attachment, trauma and the way coercion rewrites a person’s relationship to the self.

The book centers on emotional fragmentation, delayed recognition, and the practical work of rebuilding identity when language, body, and trust have all been interrupted.

Editorial Thread

This is not a generic trauma narrative. The language is literary and clinical in balance, built for readers who seek both resonance and precision.
