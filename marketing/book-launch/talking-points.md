# Talking Points

## Core Message

*Unfolding Origami: A Memoir* is a trauma therapist's personal story of surviving trauma, naming coercive control, and re-defining her shape after being folded inward by pain.

## 15-Second Version

My memoir, *Unfolding Origami*, is a trauma therapist's memoir about surviving trauma, naming coercive control, and folding back to yourself. Preorders are open now, and signed copies include a surprise from Loren.

## 30-Second Version

*Unfolding Origami: A Memoir* is about what trauma can do quietly - how it changes memory, identity, trust, and the body. It is also about survival, re-defining your shape, and the long process of folding back to yourself. Preorders are open now at https://wovenself.com/author.

## 60-Second Version

*Unfolding Origami* began as a way to tell the truth about what trauma and coercive control can do to a person internally. Not every wound is visible. Some experiences fold us inward, separate us from our voice, and make us question our own reality. For readers who have survived quietly, questioned themselves deeply, or are learning to trust their own instincts again, this memoir offers a personal story of trauma, survival, and return. Preorders are open now, and signed copies include a surprise from Loren.

## Do Not Overpromise

Avoid language like:

- This book will heal you.
- This book is a cure.
- This book is for everyone.
- This book explains all trauma.
- This is a clinical guide.

Preferred wording:

- This book may resonate with...
- This memoir explores...
- This story offers a personal lens on...
- Readers interested in trauma recovery may connect with...
